All the above shapes are created using a very simple algorithm. It starts with one large triangle, the outline of the shape you see above. Then it randomly selects one of 2-5 predefined ways of dividing the triangle into a number of smaller shapes. These shapes may be smaller equilateral triangles, hexagons, isoceles triangles, or rhombus, and each of these shapes has itself 2-5 predefined ways of being divided into smaller shapes. After the first triangle is divided, each of smaller component shapes is divided, and each of those components is divided, and so on, usually 7-12 layers deep.
When I first this up, I was amazed by the results. I think it's very interesting how such a simple algorithm can produce such intricate and unique designs.
